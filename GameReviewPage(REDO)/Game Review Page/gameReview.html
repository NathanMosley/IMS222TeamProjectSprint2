What makes gaming so culturally impactful? What makes them “fun”?

Games are like the ultimate adventures, giving everyone—not just children—the ability to adopt new personas and escape the everyday. What draws us back again and time again is the rush, happiness, and sense of achievement.

Want to expand your gaming horizons? Here are some games and series we personally recommend in terms of both quality and enjoyability!

Portal 2

Portal 2 takes place in an indeterminate amount of time. Somewhere between 50 and 50,000 years after the events of Portal, which saw Chell destroying the sentient computer GLaDOS, and landing outside the facility. Only to be dragged back inside of Aperture Science, where she was ultimately placed in a Relaxation Chamber by Doug Rattmann for the indefinite future.

A Hat In Time

In A Hat in Time you play as a tiny space-traveling girl with a big top hat. Her adventure is halted when all her fuel, the Time Pieces, is lost and scattered across a nearby planet. Hat Kid must now jump, fight and stitch new hats to make her way into every nook and cranny of this new world in order to restore her fuel and resume her journey. On her way, she’ll bump into the menacing Mafia of Cooks, the goofy birds of Dead Bird Studio, the spooky shadows of Subcon Forest, and more!

Why We Play

If you like playing 3D platformers, this one is a must play. Each land is unique in both appearance and usage of mechanics, letting this world feel more alive and vibrant. Each land has a different objective to fulfill by playing the levels, making sure the player isn’t bored. Also, each level itself has its own story, to varying degrees. While this sounds complex, the game balances it with laughs and overall silliness to make an amazing game.

Owlboy

Explore a vibrantly crafted pixel world in this flying adventure platformer. Being a mute, Otus struggles living up to the expectations of owl-hood. Things spiral from bad to worse with the sudden appearance of sky pirates.What follows is a journey through monster infested ruins, with unexpected encounters, and burdens no-one should have to bear. Forest, and more!

Professor Layton and the Curious Village

Professor Layton and the Curious Village is the first game in the Professor Layton series. It follows Professor Layton and Luke on their quest to find the Golden Apple, a mysterious inheritance hidden somewhere in the village of St. Mystere.

Why We Play

Like other games on this list, Professor Layton and The Curious Village is a part of a series. But this is a game we want to talk about exclusively. If you’re into solving puzzles, this is an absolute must-play. And it’s great for gamers of all experiences, as the mechanics are easy, and it’s mostly a virtual novel. The story is quirky and interesting, and the puzzles you can see at literally every corner of the game are fascinating. One time you might find a brain teaser that makes you think a little. The next moment you might find yourself staring at your screen for hours at the same puzzle, it’s so tough!

Resident Evil

On July 24, 1998, when a series of bizarre murders occur on the outskirts of the fictional Midwestern town of Raccoon City, the Raccoon City Police Department's S.T.A.R.S. team is assigned to investigate. After contact with Bravo Team is lost, Alpha Team is sent to investigate their disappearance.

Why We Play

Resident Evil is so good in our opinion, we can’t just mention one. The first Resident Evil game was a pioneer in the survival horror genre, sparking the creation of over 8 games! We’ve included the trailer to the very first, original game, as it’s what started it all. The first two pictures show some of the early games, which all had similar gameplay (non-over-the-shoulder third person camera, tank controls, etc.). The third picture is of the newest installment, the RE4 remake. It’s a game that shows just how far this series has come, and that it has stood the test of time. Even if there were a few mishaps along the way… So if you like horror and a challenge, give any of the games a shot! No need to play every one consecutively to have a good time. Another great thing about this series!

Batman Arkham

Battle through atman: Arkham Asylum as Batman struggles to overcome an elaborate plot by The Joker to seize control of Arkham Asylum and trap Batman inside with his enemies. Then in Batman: Arkham City, fight your way through the new super prison organized by Hugo Strange and attempt to unravel the mystery surrounding his ‘Protocol 10’ plans. And finally in Arkham Knight, on Halloween, Scarecrow forces the civilian evacuation of Gotham City after threatening to unleash his potent new fear toxin.

Why We Play

The highlighted comment reigns true for one of us over at G16… Becoming a Batman fan through these games. Trust us, it will–Get ready to pull some money together to buy comics and merchandise, baby! Anywho, this series really defined the superhero genre in video games. It pioneered the freeflow combat style, only getting more polished throughout each installment. Every game in the series does a fantastic job of making you feel like the Batman. The story is captivating, with many Batman villains to defeat. Lastly, Arkham Knight is a game that has really stood the test of time graphics-wise. It’s kind of hard to believe it came out in 2015 with how good it still looks. Seriously, go check it out!
